Jon Huntsman Biography

Huntsman was born in the California town of Redwood City. His mother, Karen Huntsman, was the daughter of LDS Church apostle David B. Haight and his father, Jon Huntsman Sr., was a business executive who founded the Huntsman Corporation, which manufactured generic Styrofoam cartons for McDonald’s and other fast-food companies and was one of the largest petrochemical companies in the United States, making him a billionaire. He is the youngest of eight siblings.

He attended Highland High School in Salt Lake City but dropped out before graduating to pursue a career as a Keyboardist in a rock band. He enrolled at the University of Utah and joined the Sigma Chi fraternity. He worked as a missionary for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in Taiwan for two years before transferring to the University of Pennsylvania, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts in International politics.

Career

During Reagan’s presidency, Huntsman worked as an assistant in the White House. During Bush’s presidency, he was a Deputy United States Trade Representative; however, he resigned to run for governor of Utah on the Republican ticket.

He was elected governor in 2004 and re-elected for four more years. Later, during Obama’s presidency, he was appointed US Ambassador to China, and he resigned in 2011 to run for president, but he dropped out in 2012 with an endorsement for candidate Mitt Romney. In 2014, he was appointed chairman of the Atlantic Council.

He was a Huntsman Corporation executive, chairman of the Huntsman Cancer Foundation, and CEO of Huntsman Family Holdings Company. He was named Chairman of the Huntsman Cancer Institute, which his father founded.

He was appointed to the board of directors of Ford Motor Company in 2012, and later to the board of Caterpillar Inc. He founded the Pacific Council on International Policy and previously served on the boards of the Brookings Institution, the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, the Asia Society in New York, and the National Bureau of Asian Research.

Jon Huntsman Achievements and Awards

Huntsman has received 11 honorary doctorate degrees, including an Honorary Doctorate of Public Service from Westminster College and an Honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters from the University of Utah. Other honorary doctorates include those from the University of Pennsylvania, Southern New Hampshire University, the University of Washington, the University of Arizona, Utah State University, and the University of Wisconsin. The BSA bestowed upon him the Distinguished Eagle Scout Award.
