Who is  Melora Hardin Young?

Melora Hardin is an American actress and singer best known for her roles as Jan Levinson on NBC’s The Office, Trudy Monk on USA Network’s Monk, and Tammy Cashman on Amazon Prime Video’s Transparent, for which she received a Primetime Emmy nomination. Hardin plays as magazine editor-in-chief Jacqueline Carlyle in The Bold Type, a Freeform comedy-drama that premiered in July 2017.

Childhood

Melora Hardin was born in Houston, Texas on June 29, 1967, to Diane and Jerry Hardin. She treasured her childhood memories of growing up with her brother Shawn Hardin before moving to California when she was four years old. She was born with acting abilities, having grown up in an acting family, and she has been in front of the camera since her undergraduate days in New York. When Hardin was five years old, her family moved to San Francisco, California, where she attended Patrick Henry Junior High School and Ulysses S. Grant High School in the San Fernando Valley. She earned her schooling at Sarah Lawrence College.
