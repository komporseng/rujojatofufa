What was Dallas Cowboys original name?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, September 16, 2024

The Dallas Cowboys, one of the most storied and successful franchises in the history of the National Football League (NFL), have a rich and intriguing origin that dates back to the team's inception in 1960.

But the team's initial name was not the iconic "Dallas Cowboys" that we know today.

The Birth of a Franchise

The Dallas Cowboys were founded in 1960 as an expansion team in the NFL.

The team's inception was the result of a successful bid led by Clint Murchison Jr., a Texas oil magnate, who was determined to bring professional football to the thriving city of Dallas.

Murchison's vision and determination laid the foundation for what would eventually become one of the most beloved and celebrated sports franchises in the world.

The Original Name: The Dallas Steers

Contrary to popular belief, the Dallas Cowboys were not originally named as such. In fact, when the team was first established, it was christened as the "Dallas Steers." The name "Steers" was a nod to the livestock and ranching heritage that is deeply ingrained in the culture and history of Texas. However, this moniker was short-lived and would soon undergo a significant transformation.

The Transformation to the Dallas Rangers

Shortly after the team was introduced as the Dallas Steers, there was a shift in the choice of name. In 1960, the franchise rebranded itself as the "Dallas Rangers."

This change was influenced by the Texas Rangers, a legendary law enforcement agency that holds a revered status in the annals of Texas history.

The name "Rangers" reflected the team's aspiration to embody the spirit of resilience, determination, and fearlessness that the Texas Rangers epitomized.

The Emergence of the Dallas Cowboys

While the Dallas Rangers seemed to resonate with the local community, it wasn't long before the franchise underwent another pivotal transformation.

In 1960, the team's general manager, Tex Schramm, recognized the need for a name that would capture the imagination of the fans and stand the test of time.

As a result, the franchise ultimately settled on the name "Dallas Cowboys."

The Significance of the Name

The adoption of the name "Dallas Cowboys" marked a turning point in the history of the franchise.

The term "Cowboys" not only paid homage to the iconic cowboys and cattle ranching culture of Texas but also encapsulated the spirit of independence, resilience, and rugged determination that are synonymous with the state.

This name resonated deeply with the people of Dallas and Texas as a whole, forging an enduring connection between the team and its passionate fan base.

Legacy and Impact

Since embracing the name "Dallas Cowboys," the franchise has gone on to achieve unparalleled success and has become an indelible part of American sports culture.

The team's legacy is defined by a rich tapestry of accomplishments, including multiple Super Bowl victories, numerous division championships, and a roster of legendary players and coaches.

The iconic star emblem of the Dallas Cowboys has become a symbol of excellence, tradition, and unwavering commitment to greatness.

 

To sum things up, The original name of the Dallas Cowboys, the "Dallas Steers," and the subsequent transformation to the "Dallas Rangers" before ultimately settling on the timeless and evocative "Dallas Cowboys" reflects the team's journey in the NFL.

The evolution of the team's name mirrors the evolution of the franchise itself, encapsulating the spirit and identity of a team that has left an indelible mark on the world of sports.
