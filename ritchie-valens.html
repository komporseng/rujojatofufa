How is the career of Ritchie Valens?

  • Ritchie began his professional life as a guitarist and singer. He frequently added new riffs and improvised the words to the music he played. He was referred to as “the Little Richard of San Fernando” by his friends and classmates because of his high school fame.
  • Bob Keane, the proprietor of Del-Fi Records, caught him performing at a San Fernando theater. Keane was thrilled with his performance and invited him to do an audition at his house. In his basement, Keane maintained a small recording studio.
  • On May 27, 1958, Ritchie had his record label audition and then agreed to a contract. He adopted the name “Ritchie” for himself because there were other Richards in the area at the time. In addition, he changed his last name, Valenzuela, to Valens.
  • Rene Hall, Carol Kaye, and Earl Palmer were among the musicians Ritchie assembled in the recording studio. The song “Come on, Let’s Go” was his debut recording. A couple of days after the recording, the song was made public.
  • The song “Donna” was on his third and last album. At the time, it received a gold disc and sold a million copies. He left high school in the fall of 1958 to focus on his music career.
