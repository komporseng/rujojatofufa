Who are the Top Football Players Born in Texas?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, June 4, 2024

Texas is known for many things, including its vast landscapes, vibrant culture, and love for football. The Lone Star State has produced some of the greatest football players of all time. From high school legends to NFL superstars, Texas has a rich history of nurturing football talent. In this article, we will explore the top football players born in Texas and their impact on the game.

Earl Campbell

One of the most iconic football players from Texas is Earl Campbell. Born in Tyler, Texas, Campbell had a remarkable career both in college and the NFL. He played college football for the University of Texas, where he won the Heisman Trophy in 1977. Campbell then went on to have a successful professional career with the Houston Oilers and the New Orleans Saints. Known for his powerful running style, Campbell was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 1991.

Tom Landry

While not a player, Tom Landry is a significant figure in Texas football history. Born in Mission, Texas, Landry was a legendary coach for the Dallas Cowboys. He revolutionized the game with his innovative strategies and led the Cowboys to two Super Bowl victories. Landry's career spanned over three decades, and he is widely regarded as one of the greatest coaches in NFL history.

Earl Thomas III

Earl Thomas III is a standout safety who was born in Orange, Texas. He played college football at the University of Texas, where he earned All-American honors. Thomas was selected by the Seattle Seahawks in the first round of the 2010 NFL Draft. He quickly established himself as one of the best safeties in the league, earning multiple Pro Bowl selections and helping the Seahawks win Super Bowl XLVIII.

Drew Brees

Drew Brees, born in Austin, Texas, is one of the most accomplished quarterbacks in NFL history. He played college football at Purdue University before being drafted by the San Diego Chargers in 2001. Brees later joined the New Orleans Saints, where he had his greatest success. He led the Saints to victory in Super Bowl XLIV and holds numerous NFL records, including most career passing yards and completions.

LaDainian Tomlinson

LaDainian Tomlinson, born in Rosebud, Texas, was a dominant running back during his playing days. He attended Texas Christian University, where he set numerous NCAA records and won the Heisman Trophy in 2000. Tomlinson was selected by the San Diego Chargers in the 2001 NFL Draft and went on to have a stellar career. He was a five-time Pro Bowler and was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2017.

Von Miller

Von Miller, born in DeSoto, Texas, is one of the most feared pass rushers in the NFL. He played college football at Texas A&M University before being drafted by the Denver Broncos in 2011. Miller quickly made an impact in the league, earning Pro Bowl selections and being named Super Bowl MVP in 2016. His speed, agility, and relentless pursuit of quarterbacks make him one of the top defensive players in the game.

Texas has been a breeding ground for top football talent for decades. The state has produced legendary players like Earl Campbell, Tom Landry, Drew Brees, and many more. These athletes have left an indelible mark on the game and have inspired countless young football players across the state. As football continues to thrive in Texas, we can expect to see even more exceptional talent emerge from the Lone Star State in the future.
